The 5 Sessions

1 live kickoff + 4 self-paced modules. Complete all 5 to earn your competency verification.

01
Live Session 45–60 minutes
Kickoff: The AI Landscape & Expectations
What AI tools exist, what they're good at, what they struggle with, and what ATO expects from you.
▾

Part 1: The AI Landscape

  • Overview of current AI tools: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot
  • What they're good at: drafting, summarizing, brainstorming, organizing, research
  • What they struggle with: math, real-time info, nuanced judgment, perfect accuracy
  • The "smart intern" mental model — useful and fast, but always needs review
  • Live demo: same prompt in two different tools to see how outputs vary

Part 2: Expectations & Goals

  • What "AI-first" means at ATO: reach for AI as a starting point, not an afterthought
  • This is part of what clients are paying for — they expect AI-ready team members
  • Timeline and deadlines for self-paced sessions
  • Introduction to the competency check: what it is and what "passing" looks like

Part 3: Privacy & Client Guidelines

  • What data is safe to put into AI tools (and what isn't)
  • Client confidentiality rules — never paste passwords, financial data, or sensitive info
  • When and how to tell a client you used AI
  • What to do if a client asks you not to use AI
Output

No deliverable. This session is about alignment, information, and Q&A.

02
Self-Paced ~45 minutes
Prompting Foundations
Go from "I typed something and got a weird answer" to "I can consistently get useful results."
▾

What You'll Learn

  • The anatomy of a good prompt: role, context, task, format, constraints
  • Before/after examples using real ATO work scenarios
  • The "be specific" principle — vague in = vague out
  • How to iterate when the first result isn't right
  • Giving feedback mid-conversation: "make it shorter," "more formal," "try a different angle"

Why This Matters at ATO

  • Good prompting is the difference between AI saving you 30 minutes or wasting 30 minutes
  • Clients will see the quality of your AI-assisted output — it needs to be client-ready
Practice Exercise

Take 3 common work tasks and write prompts for each. Run them in any free AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini). Save the outputs.

03
Self-Paced ~30 minutes
Getting Comfortable With Free Tools
Build the AI habit using what's freely available — before paid tools are provisioned.
▾

What You'll Do

  • Try at least two free AI tools (ChatGPT free, Claude free, Gemini)
  • Run the same prompt in both and compare results — notice where they differ
  • Explore use cases: draft an email, summarize an article, plan a task list, brainstorm ideas
  • Start forming a preference — which tool feels most natural? (No wrong answer.)
  • Learn each tool's basics: starting a new chat, uploading a file, adjusting settings
Practice Exercise

Pick a real task from your day (or make one up) and complete it using two different AI tools. Write a short note on which tool you preferred and why.

04
Self-Paced ~1 hour
AI for Real Work Tasks
Apply AI to the actual work you'll be doing — email, research, planning, drafting, data, and editing.
▾

Core Work Areas

  • Email triage & drafting: sorting by urgency, writing replies, summarizing threads
  • Research & summarization: pulling together info on a topic, condensing long documents
  • Task planning: turning a messy list into an organized plan with priorities
  • Communication drafting: writing updates, messages, or briefs in a professional tone
  • Light data work: organizing info, pulling key points from a spreadsheet or list
  • Editing & improving: using AI to review and improve your own writing
  • Client communication & AI transparency: three-scenario framework for working with clients
Practice Exercise

Complete at least 3 of the tasks above using AI. Save the outputs. Be ready to talk about what worked and what you had to fix.

05
Capstone Output required
Putting It Together — Competency Check
Everything from Sessions 1–4 comes together. Your output here is what gets reviewed.
▾

What to Prepare

  • Review what you've learned: prompting, tool familiarity, real task application
  • Think about how AI fits into the kind of work you'll be doing at ATO
  • Consider: what are the 3–5 tasks where AI saves you the most time?

Final Output (Required)

  • How you'd use AI in your role: Pick 3–5 specific tasks and show the prompts you'd use
  • A sample deliverable: One real piece of work you completed using AI during training
  • What you learned: What surprised you, what didn't work, what you'd want to learn more about
  • Client communication reflection: How you'd handle AI transparency with a client

What "Passing" Looks Like

  • Shows you can use AI for real work (not generic copy-paste)
  • Tasks are relevant to your role with prompts that make sense
  • The sample deliverable is something a client would find useful
  • You can articulate what worked and what you'd improve
  • Privacy guidelines are followed throughout
Deliverable

A short document or presentation (1–2 pages max) covering all three areas above. This gets shared during a brief review with your point person.
