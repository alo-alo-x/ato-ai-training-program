Session 1: Kickoff

Get oriented and understand the AI landscape

Live Session 45–60 minutes

Before the Session

This is a conversation, not a lecture. Keep it casual.

Facilitator Prep Checklist

  • Confirm team member completed Pre-Work
  • Have two AI tools open for demo (Claude free + ChatGPT free)
  • Have a sample prompt ready
  • Send calendar invite with link 24 hours ahead
  • Have training timeline ready

Part 1: The AI Landscape (15 min)

Opening Question

Start with: "Have you used any AI tools before? What was your experience like?"

The Big Three (and Friends)

  • ChatGPT (OpenAI)
  • Claude (Anthropic — ATO's recommended)
  • Gemini (Google)
  • Copilot (Microsoft)
  • Others

Key Point: "You don't need to learn all of these. We'll focus on Claude..."

What AI Is Good At

  • Drafting
  • Summarizing
  • Brainstorming
  • Research
  • Planning
  • Reformatting

What AI Struggles With

  • Math
  • Real-time information
  • Nuanced judgment
  • Perfect accuracy
  • Personal context

Mental Model

"Think of AI like a really smart, really fast intern."

Live Demo (5 min)

Run the same prompt in two tools side by side. Demo prompt: "An executive assistant is preparing their client for an upcoming client meeting. Draft a brief pre-meeting summary."

Compare and discuss the outputs—structure, tone, detail, and accuracy.

Part 2: Expectations and Goals (15 min)

What "AI-First" Means

We're not asking you to replace your judgment with AI. We're asking you to use AI as a tool to work faster, smarter, and more strategically. This isn't optional—it's how ATO operates.

Why This Matters

Every ATO client receives a team member who is AI-enabled and equipped to deliver faster, higher-quality work.

Training Timeline

You're in a 5-session program:

  1. Session 1 (today): Kickoff
  2. Session 2: Prompting Foundations
  3. Session 3: Getting Comfortable With Free Tools
  4. Session 4: Building Your Toolkit
  5. Session 5: Integration & Next Steps

Competency Check

We'll assess your readiness through practical exercises and real-world scenarios. You'll be ready for Day 1 with clients.

Part 3: Privacy, Confidentiality, and Client Guidelines (10 min)

What's OK to Use AI For

  • General tasks (drafting, summarizing, brainstorming)
  • Your own notes and planning
  • Public information
  • De-identified examples

What's NOT OK

  • Passwords
  • Financial data
  • Sensitive personal information
  • Confidential business data
  • Client proprietary information

Client Communication

Client asks about AI use: Be honest, and frame it positively. Example: "I use AI to draft this faster and better, then I review and adapt it for your specific situation."

Client says "don't use AI": Respect that immediately. No exceptions.

ATO's Position

AI enhances your judgment. It doesn't replace it. You are always the decision-maker.

Wrap-Up and Next Steps (5 min)

3 Key Takeaways

  1. AI is a tool—one of many you'll use to work faster and smarter.
  2. The big tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) all do similar things. We focus on Claude.
  3. Privacy and client preferences come first. Always.

Action Items Before Session 2

  • Create a free Claude account at claude.ai
  • Try one simple prompt: "Draft a professional email asking for a 15-minute meeting"
  • Note any questions or surprises

Final Word

You're stepping into a skill that's going to set you apart. The fact that you're here means you're ready. Let's build this together.

Facilitator Notes

FAQ & Suggested Responses

Question Suggested Response
"Is AI going to replace my job?" "No. But people who use AI well might replace people who don't. You're learning to be the first group."
"What if the AI gets it wrong?" "Great question. That's why you're here. You'll learn to review, edit, and verify. AI is a draft engine, not a truth engine."
"Do I have to use Claude?" "We recommend Claude and it's what you'll train on. But you'll learn principles that apply to any tool."
"Is this ethical?" "Yes, if we're transparent with clients and we use it responsibly. That's what this training is about."
"I've never done this before." "Perfect. No prior AI experience needed. We're building it from the ground up."

Tips for Different Participant Types

  • Nervous participants: Remind them this is low-stakes. Mistakes are how we learn. Start with small, safe prompts.
  • Bored participants: Move fast through basics. Give them harder scenarios early. Show real client use cases.
  • Inexperienced tech participants: Use plain language. Avoid jargon. Spend extra time on the demo. Offer a follow-up chat if needed.
